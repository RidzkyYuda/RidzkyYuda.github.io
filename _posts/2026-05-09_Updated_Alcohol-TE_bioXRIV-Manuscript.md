We just updated our bioRXIV preprint manuscript with the title **Chronic alcohol exposure drives inflammaging and transposon derepression in hematopoietic stem and progenitor cells**. This revision is an update of the initial manuscript submitted to bioRXIV on December 2025. The manuscript contains several key updates as follow (1) LPS inflammation challenge and EdU incorporation assay to show the impact of previous ethanol exposure on HSPC reactivation towards inflammatory stimulus, presented in Figure 2. (2) Additional data points to strengthen the robustness of Ki67 and gamma H2AX meassurement in Figure 1. (3) Re-analysis of the human xenotransplanted HSPC's scRNA-seq to incorporate differences in the cohort 1 and cohort 2 presented in Figure 6. (4) Added the analysis of transposable elements from bulk RNA-seq data of vitro cultured human CD34+ cells exposed to ethanol, presented in Figure 7. (5) Added the analysis of transposable elements in human HSC and MPPs from cohort 1 and 2 presented in Figure 7 (6) Added the GO analysis of total and TE-overlapping OCRs presented in Figure 7 and Supplemental Figure 7 (7) Added motif analysis from total and TE-overlapping OCRs presented in Figure 7 (8) Added RT-qPCR data of IFNA1, IFNB1, IFNG from alcohol-exposed human CD34+ cell culture in Figure 7. (9) Tightening discussion part to focus on the TE and inflammaging, and shows the recent literatures regarding TE upregulation during stress hematopoiesis
